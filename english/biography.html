Peng Lin

Peng Lin is a Chinese pianist who is considered as a rising star among the young generation of pianists. She was born and raised on Gulangyu, “the music island” in Xiamen, China. On this island, she began to play piano at the age of four and made her orchestral debut with Mozart Concerto K. 467 at the age of nine. By the age of eleven, she had already won five first prizes in national piano competitions. Ms. Lin moved to New York at the age fifteen when she was offered full scholarship to attend the Pre-College of the Juilliard School. Ms. Lin subsequently completed her Bachelor of Music and Master of Music degrees at the Juilliard School, under the tutorage of Dr. Yoheved Kaplinsky and Mr. Hung-Kuan Chen. In May 2020, Ms. Lin received her Master of Musical Art degree from Yale School of Music where she studied with Mr. Hung-Kuan Chen and Mr. Peter Serkin. Ms. Lin received Artist Diploma from the Royal College of Music, and is now based in London.

She was the first prize winner of the 2012 Rhodes International Piano Competition and has garnered top honours at numerous international competitions, including the Bach Special Prize in the 2016 Rina Sala Gallo International Piano Competition, the second prize of the 2009 Louisiana International Piano Competition, and the third prize and Chopin Prize of the 8th International Competition for Young Pianists “Arthur Rubinstein in Memoriam”.

Ms. Lin has performed throughout China, USA, Russia, Canada, Germany, Greece, Japan, Italy, Netherland, Poland, Switzerland, and the UK. She has been heard on stages of such prestigious venues as Carnegie Hall in New York, Muziekgebouw Hall in Amsterdam and Shanghai Concert Hall in Shanghai 1. She has also worked closely with members of renowned Borromeo Quartet, Miró Quartet and Brentano Quartet 1.
